# Christine Bell — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional EOS Integrator** | Entrepreneurial Operating System Implementation
Takes the visionary's vision and implements it throughout the organization. Runs leadership team meetings, sales team meetings, manages quarterly rocks, and designs growth strategies for small businesses.

---

## The Origin Story

Christine experienced the power of fractional first-hand from the CLIENT side before becoming a fractional herself. Her family's electronics company brought in a fractional Director of Marketing in 2021 — an early adopter move.

> "She did such a phenomenal job, and it really built credibility. It gave me the confidence that I could be a fractional and actually make a difference. And I'm glad I made the leap."

Under EOS, Christine's family company grew **54% in 4 years, doubling in size.**

> "We grew 54% in 4 years. We doubled the size of our company. We were looking to grow and looking to expand, and it was only because of the tools that EOS provides."

### The Connector Role (from Fireflies)
From Ford's planning meetings, Christine emerged as the connective tissue of the Cincy Voices event itself. Ford told her directly:

> "You are the connector. Nobody can do what you're doing."

She ran the event planning like an L10 — weekly meetings, clear accountability, constant communication. Her mantra: "Communicate, communicate, communicate." She pushed for diversity in moderators and panelists, and kept the entire operation on track.

---

## What Makes Christine Remarkable

### 1. She Brings Visions to Life
Christine's role as an integrator is fundamentally about translating someone else's dream into reality. She steers the ship while the visionary focuses on big ideas and relationships.

> "The visionary's in charge of the big ideas and big relationships. They drive strategy and live the culture. And the integrator is there to steer the ship, make sure everyone's rowing in the same direction, and really take the visionary's vision and implement it throughout the organization."

> "The biggest win for me, for my clients, is helping them see their vision come to life."

### 2. The Sales Sprint That Got Chills
Christine designed an aggressive sales sprint for a client whose growth had stalled. The results gave her chills — literally.

> "Oh my gosh, it's amazing when you put focus and intention on something. I just got the chills. They have until September 30th to hit their target and they're half a million short, but they've got it in the pipeline. Like they just need to close it."

### 3. She Names the Founder's Blind Spot
Christine is direct about the most common obstacle in her work: the founder themselves.

> "Many times they're their own obstacle. But if they can't see it, it will be difficult for me to make a difference."

### 4. Collaboration Over Competition — A Real Story
Christine witnessed Ford's vision for a fractional community playing out in real time. Another fractional in Cincinnati dropped out of a competitive process because they recognized the client needed an EOS integrator — Christine's specialty.

> "When he told me that, I just didn't know what to say. But that is exactly what Ford is trying to build. And that's, I think, the future."

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Core values alignment first:** Before taking a client, she checks whether the visionary and integrator can have a collaborative (not competitive) relationship
- **Vision buy-in:** If the leadership team hasn't bought into the vision, the work is exponentially harder
- **Atomic Habits principle:** "If you're off course by just 1%, how far off that actually is." She applies this to keeping clients aligned with their vision.
- **Trust = accountability:** "When you do what you say you're going to do, that builds accountability. But it's also building trust."
- **Health first:** "I want to be working now for my 80-year-old self, and I wish I were doing it when I was 20."
