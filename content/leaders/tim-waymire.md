# Tim Waymire — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Chief Development Officer, Telestai Capital** | M&A Advisory, Fractional CFO Services, Business Growth Strategy
Helps Midwest business owners build, grow, and exit their companies — with hands-on execution, not just high-level strategy.

---

## The Origin Story

Tim grew up on a farm in Dayton, Ohio, surrounded by entrepreneurs. Two family farms, a flower business, a wedding venue — hard work and building things was the family language.

> "That environment taught me about family, faith, hard work, character, doing things the right way."

He attended UC, went into management consulting, but his heart kept pulling him toward teaching. So he became a high school teacher and built an entrepreneurship program from scratch over five years — a leadership class, sports marketing, an entrepreneurship course where students built real businesses for eight months, and a strategic entrepreneurship track running the school spirit shop. He coached basketball at every level from 2nd grade through varsity assistant.

> "My dream was to build a dynasty that changed kids' lives and won a bunch of basketball games."

> "I recognize that my legacy and what I wanted to do was to help people become the best versions of themselves."

But he couldn't support his family on a teacher's salary. And one story haunted him — his grandfather spent 34 years building a flower business from scratch and sold it for pennies on the dollar. Tim didn't want that to happen to other Midwest business owners. He joined Telestai Capital in August 2024.

> "My grandpa spent 34 years building a flower business from scratch... he pretty much sold the business for pennies on the dollar. I don't want to see that happen to other Midwest business owners."

---

## What Makes Tim Remarkable

### 1. Teacher-Turned-Dealmaker with a Rare Heart
Tim built an entrepreneurship program from scratch at a high school — then brought that same builder and mentor DNA into M&A. The teaching instinct never left. He doesn't just advise business owners; he develops them.

> "Our scars make us sensitive to those things and make us want to help somebody through that."

### 2. Results That Make Clients Cry
Tim's impact isn't measured in spreadsheets alone. One client broke down in tears at dinner — the kind of moment most fractional partners never experience.

> "The owner broke down in tears at dinner... 'This is the first time in my 35-year business career that I have a strategic partner that can actually help me get to where I'm trying to go.'"

### 3. The Grandfather Wound Drives Everything
The image of his grandfather selling a 34-year flower business for pennies is Tim's emotional engine. It fuels every client engagement and every late-night strategy session.

### 4. Boots-on-the-Ground Fractional Model
Weekly cadences, hands-on execution, not just slide decks and quarterly check-ins. Tim and his team do the work — something he says most fractional CFO firms won't touch.

> "The traditional fractional CFO wants to be very high level strategy. We also help with the execution — something I've yet to see a fractional CFO company do."

### 5. Rapid Growth in Year One
In roughly one year at Telestai Capital: 4 large accounts, acquisitions in Kentucky and Wisconsin, $500K+ in cost savings identified, and $1.5M in top-line revenue added for clients.

> "The owner called us and said, 'You guys have been a godsend. In fact, I want to give you guys a raise because you guys are that good.'"

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Get your hands dirty:** Execution matters more than strategy decks
- **Build to exit, build to sell:** Help owners think about the endgame from day one
- **Genuinely care:** Long-term personal and professional success, not transactional engagements
- **Think relational, not transactional:** The relationship outlasts any single deal
- **Mentorship is the throughline:** From coaching 2nd graders to advising business owners, the work is the same — helping people become the best versions of themselves
