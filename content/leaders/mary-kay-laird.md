# Mary Kay Laird — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Business Development Director** | Supporting Strategies
Sells outsourced accounting services to growing companies — but unlike most salespeople, she spent 17 years running operations as a COO, so she has lived every pain point she discusses with prospects.

---

## The Origin Story

Mary Kay's career is a study in reinvention. She started at Deloitte as an auditor, then moved to Fidelity to manage 401(k) plans. When she went back as a contractor, she found herself on the frontier of something unexpected:

> "I was working from home in 2002. Writing content for Fidelity's first website from home using dial-up internet."

From there, she spent 17 years at 4th Street Performance Partners, rising from business manager to COO — handling recruiting, career development, compensation analysis, and SEC compliance. That operator's instinct now fuels everything about how she sells.

---

## What Makes Mary Kay Remarkable

### 1. The Operator Who Sells
Mary Kay didn't come to business development from a sales background. She came from running a business. Seventeen years as COO means she has sat in every seat her prospects occupy — managing people, navigating compliance, cleaning up processes. When she talks about the pain of messy books or the fear of giving up control, she's speaking from lived experience, not a pitch deck.

> "I don't just think Supporting Strategies has a good product because they told me so. I think it's a good product because from what I've seen."

### 2. Anti-Salesperson Sales Approach
Mary Kay doesn't push. She plants seeds and focuses on genuine fit. Her method is rooted in generosity — give first, build trust, let the relationship reveal whether the service is right.

> "I am not a hardcore salesperson, so I really focus on — does our service help your company? And it does 90% of the time."

### 3. Pattern Recognition for Client Readiness
After years of screening prospects, Mary Kay has learned that industry fit matters less than mindset. She looks for three signals: willingness to give up control, the ability to admit they need help, and availability to collaborate through the transition.

> "I'm really having a hard time identifying our ideal client because it's really the mindset of the owner."

> "If they are a control freak, that doesn't work. They need to admit that they need help."

### 4. Career Chameleon
Auditor. 401(k) manager. Website content writer. COO. Business development director. Each chapter built on the last, but none of them were obvious next steps at the time.

> "I realized how important it is just to not stay in your lane always."

### 5. Community Builder
Mary Kay doesn't just network locally — she evangelizes Cincinnati's fractional leadership community to colleagues across the country. She sees the density of talent in the region and actively works to connect people and build visibility for it.

---

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Give first:** Gives a lot, gets a lot back — generosity as a business development strategy
- **Loves the chaos:** Thrives in the messy middle of cleaning up books and getting systems in order
- **Mindset over industry:** Screens for client readiness and willingness to change, not vertical fit
- **Authenticity above all:** Values genuine connection over polished sales technique
- **Revenue consistency matters:** Understands that clean financials signal stability to investors and buyers

> "I love the chaos. When we get them over that hump — I love seeing it when everything's in its place."

> "If your revenue flow isn't consistent, private equity is going to think that you have some kind of volatility."
