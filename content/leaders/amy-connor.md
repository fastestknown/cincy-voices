# Amy Connor — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional CMO / Founder of CMO on Loan** | Marketing Strategy, Brand Building, Mid-Market Growth
Works primarily with mid-market companies ($10-75M) that need senior marketing firepower but not a full-time CMO — bringing P&G and Luxottica-level expertise to businesses that could never otherwise access it.

---

## The Origin Story

Amy's career began with a two-year internship at IAMS Company in market research — an experience that changed the trajectory of her life.

> "Interning changed the trajectory of my life at IAMS and I'm so appreciative of those people."

She earned her MBA from the University of Dayton, and when IAMS was acquired by Procter & Gamble in 1999, Amy moved into the P&G machine — working on Pepto-Bismol, Metamucil, and the pre-launch team for Align. In 2010, she made the leap to Luxottica, where she worked her way up to VP of Marketing for LensCrafters North America.

In 2016, facing an international relocation she didn't want, Amy stepped away from corporate. Business owners she knew gave her the nudge:

> "I could use your firepower, I just don't think I need it every day."

> "My career was not necessarily the plan I intended."

---

## What Makes Amy Remarkable

### 1. Corporate Pedigree Deployed for the Underserved Mid-Market
Amy spent nearly two decades inside P&G and Luxottica — two of the most rigorous marketing organizations on the planet. Now she brings that caliber of strategic thinking to $10-75M companies that would never have access to it otherwise.

> "We are not trying to deliver marketing. Our purpose is to help fuel mid-market company growth."

### 2. The "Big S" Model — Build, Implement, Graduate
Amy's engagement model is deliberately self-terminating. She builds marketing capability, implements it alongside the team, then graduates the client to independence. She works herself out of a job — on purpose.

> "If they don't get it, it actually doesn't matter if I get it."

She provides tools clients keep and use independently. The goal is marketing confidence inside the company, not a dependency on her.

### 3. Nine-Year Client Relationships
Despite a model designed to graduate clients, Amy's first client — Modernize, from 2017 — still works with her nine years later. The relationships endure because the trust runs deep.

> "When we get that trust level going, we can do amazing things together."

She caps her practice at 12-14 clients, ensuring every engagement gets the attention it deserves.

### 4. The Launching Pad — Paying It Forward
Amy runs an internship program called "Launching Pad" specifically for college freshmen and sophomores — paying forward what IAMS gave her decades ago. She knows what that early career exposure meant for her trajectory and is determined to create it for others.

> "Interning changed the trajectory of my life at IAMS and I'm so appreciative of those people."

### 5. A Team Built on Trust
Amy has assembled a team of 5 contractors drawn from former P&G and Luxottica colleagues — people she has worked with, trusts deeply, and whose capabilities she knows firsthand.

---

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Capability over dependency:** The purpose is building marketing confidence inside companies, not delivering marketing for them
- **Normal people words:** Strips the jargon. If the client doesn't get it, the strategy doesn't matter
- **Respect what exists:** "Don't break something that's older than you, but breathe a little life into it."
- **Deliberate scale:** Caps at 12-14 clients to maintain quality and depth
- **Corporate love, fractional calling:** "I loved being in corporate. But I can't picture going back now because I feel like I'm helping people in a different way."
