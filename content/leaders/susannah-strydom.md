# Susannah Strydom — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional Creative/Brand Strategy Leader** | Design Strategy, Brand Fundamentals, AI Exploration
Former P&G executive. Globally experienced (South Africa, Singapore/Asia, US). Husband was ranked #3 creative director globally in Asia. Brings elite-level brand and design thinking to mid-market companies that have never had it.

---

## The Origin Story

### Apartheid South Africa
Susannah grew up in apartheid-era South Africa. That experience baked empathy into her DNA.

> "The story from my childhood that shaped me today is firstly, I grew up in South Africa. I grew up in apartheid South Africa. So really having a strong empathy for humans is very much part of my DNA."

### Leaving P&G — Freedom and Fear
Susannah left P&G (referred to as "big corporate" and "PNG" in conversation) after what she describes as a traumatic departure. She chose not to go back.

> "My hope is Ford that I don't have to sell my soul to a big corporate again."

Leaving was exhilarating but also heavy. She had to rebuild. Her first fractional client is Jividan — a 3-month contract, 2 days per week. Pepsi reached out for full-time work, but she chose consulting. She wants to prove the fractional model can work at the highest level.

### The Weight of Responsibility
Susannah's husband, formerly ranked #3 creative director globally in Asia, moved to the US and is now earning minimum wage while getting his degree to work in addiction counseling. Susannah is putting him through college.

> "I feel a lot of responsibility, to be honest. It's a very heavy feeling for me. And I'm putting my husband through college."

Her husband values loyalty above all. He hates "all talk no walk" people. He's genuine — "salt of the earth."

---

## What Makes Susannah Remarkable

### 1. She Sees What Companies Don't Have
Susannah is consistently surprised by how even large companies ($200M+) lack basic brand fundamentals. She fills a gap most companies don't even know they have.

> "It's been actually very surprising to me what they don't have. Very big businesses out there, $200 million plus — they don't have what I would call a basic brand fundamental in place. The value that someone with my skill set can bring is immeasurable."

### 2. Design Is Strategy, Not Decoration
This is her hill to die on. Design is not "making things pretty" — it's visual problem-solving through a strategic lens.

> "People have thought design is the person who makes things pretty, and that is not design. We understand how to problem solve visually, and that is through a strategic lens, that is through our innate creativity."

### 3. She's a Kid in a Candy Store with AI
Coming from P&G where IP restrictions limited what tools you could use, the fractional world has set her free to explore AI without corporate constraints.

> "When I retired from P&G, it was really a very refreshing outlook. I feel like I'm a kid in a candy store — really looking at the world of AI, not just understanding the tools, but understanding the actual business and landscape of AI."

### 4. The Pill-in-Peanut-Butter Approach
Susannah's approach to client transformation is layered. She gives clients what they asked for (the peanut butter) while embedding what they actually need (the pill).

> "The peanut butter is really their outstanding brief. And then the very heart and the crux of it is the pill in the center."

### 5. She's the Honest Voice in the Boardroom
Fractional leaders don't carry organizational baggage. Susannah leverages that freedom to say what insiders can't.

> "Fractional leaders don't come in with the baggage. So you can say something that someone ordinarily might not feel comfortable saying at the boardroom. But I think that is an expectation when you're coming in with that leadership — to say how you really see things."

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Growth through hardship:** "I try not to think of, oh, I'm in this terrible space. I rather try to think about what can I get from it, how can I improve, what is the lesson here."
- **Life understood backwards:** "I know that we only understand life looking backwards, so that's how I approach it."
- **Small wins first:** Build trust through quick deliverables before transforming strategy
- **Mentorship as gift:** Fractional leaders fill the mentorship gap that corporate streamlining has created
- **Anti-silo:** Diverse thinking and outside perspectives are non-negotiable for modern business success
- **AI optimism for creatives:** "Agents still need to market to agents. I feel like the work's just going to get even more for us."
