# Will Phillips — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional People & Culture Executive** | Strengths-Based Organizational Development
Operates strengths-first. Creates environments — cultural and operational — that allow people to thrive. Helps business owners build the team they need so they can step out of operations.

---

## The Origin Story

### The 6th Grade Moment
Will's entire philosophy traces back to a moment in 6th grade when he decided to change himself to fit his environment. It worked — but it made him miserable.

> "Something from my childhood that shaped me to be who I am today is when I was in the 6th grade, I remember thinking like, man, what I'm doing isn't working. So I'm going to change myself so that I can better fit into my environment. And I kind of did and it kind of worked, but I was also mostly miserable."

That experience created his foundational belief:

> "I believe that the number one reason why people aren't thriving is because they're not in an environment that supports them to thrive."

### The ADHD Masterclass
Will's first real job was in a high attention-to-detail environment — a terrible fit for someone with ADHD. Instead of failing, he built systems to compensate. Three years in that role became a masterclass in executive functioning.

> "If I was designing the hiring system, I wouldn't have hired me — because I was a horrible fit for it. But what I did on the job is I figured out the systems I could develop so that I could operate out of my strengths and not my weaknesses."

## What Makes Will Remarkable

### 1. The Power of Stupid Questions
Will's most distinctive insight is about the unique advantage fractional leaders have in discovery: they can ask the questions nobody else will.

> "A significant portion of the value I bring during the discovery stage is in asking stupid questions. Because the stupid questions, everyone knows the answer to them, and they're like, 'Ooh, we don't talk about that thing.' People exist in cultures and culture shapes them."

> "Fractional people come in and they get to ask stupid questions because they're not anchoring their livelihood and their identity to this organization."

### 2. The Lead Gen Firm Story — Best Value Proposition Ever
Will tells a jaw-dropping story about the most honest job pitch he ever received. A lead gen firm owner said: "You're really talented. You're not going to be here for more than 2 years. But here's what's going to happen..." Then laid out exactly how the job would make Will's career — volume, relationships, coaching — and if his clients didn't hire him away, the owner would personally make introductions.

> "If you're selling without a value proposition, you're competing on price. That's what most organizations do with their people — they think the value they provide is a paycheck. And a lot of times that's one of the least valuable things an organization does."

### 3. The Business Already Knows the Answer
Will's Fortune 500 experience taught him that teams almost always have the solution — they just can't get it assembled. The answer is scattered across people's heads in 25% pieces.

> "What I realized through that experience is that team had the solution, they just couldn't get it out. It was 25% here, 30% here, 20% here. 99% of the time, the business knows what it needs to do. And it's my job to just pull it out of people's heads and create alignment and momentum."

### 4. The Employee Tourist
Will coined a beautiful term for the fractional experience — being part of the team without having to suffer the organizational pain.

> "You get to enjoy it almost like an employee tourist. You're a part of the team without having to suffer the pain that people have to suffer as a result of certain cultural things."

### 5. Culture Misalignment Kills
Will is blunt about the most destructive thing in organizations: saying you're one kind of culture and acting like another.

> "The worst thing that can happen is misaligning culture. You say we're a family environment and then someone doesn't hit their numbers and they get fired. Well, no, really. That happens all the time because they're not a family environment."

### 6. Delegate Meaningful Responsibility
His actionable advice to business owners: stop giving tasks. Give your best employee real ownership.

> "Give your best employee some responsibility that's on your plate right now. Don't tell them, 'Do this task.' Say, 'This is your responsibility. What can I do to help you be successful in this?' I think you'll be blown away by what they can do for you."

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Strengths-first:** Every engagement starts from the belief that people thrive when their environment supports their natural strengths
- **Alignment extraction:** His job is to pull the scattered pieces of knowledge out of people's heads and create momentum
- **Passion alignment:** Ask enough questions, do what you say you'll do, employees trust you, then they communicate their passions, then you know what they'll work 10x harder on
- **Stewarding vulnerability:** "Knowing what's appropriate to share and what could damage someone's career — it's our job to just get to the truth."
- **Peeling the onion:** "Someone smart won't say the truth the first question. But then you ask the second question, you get a little bit more."
- **Authentic culture:** Don't claim to be something you're not. Figure out what your real culture is and own it.
