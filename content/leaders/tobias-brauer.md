# Tobias Brauer (Toby) — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional CMO & Boutique Design Agency Founder** | Brand Strategy, Marketing Leadership
Founded SolveStat about 2 years ago. Goes by "Toby Brower." Does fractional CMO work alongside running a boutique design agency. Background in agency leadership, managing teams of 15+.

---

## What Makes Tobias Remarkable

### 1. Brand Is Culture, Not Marketing
Tobias reframes brand as an internal operating system, not an external campaign. When other panelists discussed trust and culture, he immediately saw it through the brand lens — and redefined it.

> "A brand in my mind is not just for recruiting new customers and generating revenue. It's about building a culture for your internal people so they know how to conduct themselves according to the promises you're making as a company. They're empowered to make decisions even sometimes without talking to management because it's the thing the brand promises."

This is his signature insight — brand as the mechanism for employee empowerment and cultural alignment.

### 2. He Gets Truly Embedded
Tobias doesn't do drive-by strategy. He assimilates into client organizations — getting email credentials, making his calendar available, attending weekly meetings. He sets aside an hour every week just to talk with the CEO/founder.

> "You need to start to think of yourself as a part of the organization. You need to say, 'we are doing this' — not just 'let me give you some advice that you will deal with.'"

> "I set aside an hour every week to just talk to this person and talk about what their goals are — to get inside their head a little bit so that I know the strategies and decisions I'm making are aligned with what they want."

### 3. He's the Medicine They Need to Take
Tobias knows that fractional leaders are often brought in because something hard needs to happen. He balances honesty with cultural sensitivity.

> "Usually they bring you in because there's some kind of medicine they know they have to take. And you're the one that's going to be honest with them and help them get back on the right track."

He amplifies the voices of employees who've been fighting the good fight but lack the executive authority to be heard.

### 4. Service and Credit-Giving
As someone who's managed 15+ people throughout his career, Tobias believes that once you've reached a certain level, your job is to make everyone else shine.

> "When you're in a position where you've already got some experience and you've risen to a certain level, you sort of owe it to everybody else. You don't need accolades anymore. Go ahead and divert the successes and the attention to the people who are really doing the work."

### 5. He Knows Why People Leave
Tobias has seen the pattern over and over — people don't leave for money. They leave because they felt unheard.

> "I've seen a lot of people leave because they felt like they weren't heard. I don't think it's always money. I think it's probably rarely money."

### 6. Vulnerability = Saying "I Don't Know"
For Tobias, the most powerful form of vulnerability in business is intellectual honesty.

> "One of the places I can identify vulnerability right away is when someone has the courage to say, 'I don't know.' Instead of giving you a canned, memorized response, they go, 'You know what? I don't know, but maybe I'll try to find out.'"

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Marketing as cultural translation:** Gets the foundational reason the company exists out into the world, tying together stakeholders from every function
- **Service is the product:** "The reason anyone hires any fractional is the service that they provide. They're coming for you."
- **Risk-taking:** "People need to be less risk-averse. You're not going to get to a new peak unless you take some risk."
- **Embedding fully:** Calendar, email, credentials, weekly CEO check-ins — he becomes part of the team
- **Shaping culture through hiring:** Uses his position to recruit and develop FTEs, setting up department standards and culture
