# Amy Sheehy — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional COO** | Analytics & Operations Coaching
**Unique Model:** Clients pay a charity rather than pay Amy directly — the client gets full credit for what's effectively a donation, with the expense coming off their operating expenses.

---

## The Origin Story

Amy came up through engineering and manufacturing. Early in her career, she was a female manager at a manufacturing site — an outsider in every sense. She wasn't from that town. Most of her employees hated her. She had to earn trust the hard way.

> "Most of my employees hated me, and I needed to demonstrate what my intentions were over time to earn trust. I did it in some cases by doing the work that no one else wanted to do. I helped carry tools. I held flashlights. I did things that were boring and made work easier for other people."

That was the beginning. Over time, she used her education to help those same people advance their careers. This experience forged Amy's approach to leadership: earn trust through service, then deliver results.

---

## What Makes Amy Remarkable

### 1. The Charity Business Model
Amy's business model is unlike anyone else's in the fractional world. Clients don't pay her — they pay a charity. This isn't a gimmick; it's a deeply held value system built into her operating structure.

> "What's unique about my business — clients pay a charity rather than pay me."

### 2. She Learns Alongside Her Clients
Amy doesn't come in pretending to know everything. In one engagement, she had to learn an entirely unfamiliar discipline from the team before she could advise them. That vulnerability built trust — and the result was a **1,000 basis point increase in revenue.**

> "That team taught me their function, and then I was able to build their trust so that they could take my operational advice. That led to a 1,000 basis point increase in revenue."

### 3. She Transforms Team Culture Through Results
One of Amy's most powerful stories is about walking into a burned-out team doing a new product startup. They were exhausted. They wouldn't make eye contact with each other. She started a weekly L10-style review, pretended not to see the tension, stayed positive, and focused on incremental data-driven wins.

> "When I started, the team would not make eye contact with each other. Within a few weeks, people were telling jokes and celebrating and looking at each other. It changed the culture of the team."

### 4. She Makes the Invisible Visible
Amy's work with analytics doesn't just improve numbers — it transforms people's self-perception. One client, a new business leader, was working incredibly hard but couldn't see her own progress. Amy put together analytics that showed her trajectory.

> "I will never forget her almost breaking down in tears saying, 'I didn't know how well I was doing.' A year later she won an award from the company for her performance."

## Signature Client Impact Stories

### The 75% Wait Time Reduction
A light manufacturer felt constantly behind. Their client was unhappy, and it was affecting sales across the organization. Amy reframed how they managed performance — looking at behaviors and daily choices rather than just outputs. Result: **75% reduction in wait time**, which then unlocked the sales team to sell at a higher rate.

### The KPI Misalignment Fix
Two verticals within the same company were fighting each other because their KPIs appeared to conflict. Neither set of metrics was complete. Amy realigned how they tracked results, and both organizations started collaborating instead of competing.

### The Vulnerable Leader
A new business leader who wasn't seeing her own progress, working incredibly hard but feeling like she was failing. Amy's analytics showed her the truth. Tears. Then, a year later, an award from the company.

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Start with service:** "You start by asking the good questions. And as much as it's reasonable, do the work yourself."
- **Hands-on entry:** Spends time early in engagements working alongside people at every level, asking: "What makes this difficult for you? What do you wish was different? I am here at your service."
- **Circle of trust:** "I've always been blown away — as an outside party, the things that people will tell you when you're outside the culture, you're outside of the circle of trust."
- **Making others look good:** "As a fractional executive, your job is to make the client look good."
- **Tailoring communication:** Learned to start with emotion when talking to sales teams, and start with data when talking to engineers.
