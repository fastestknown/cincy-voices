# Dan Keck — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional CFO** | Financial Leadership for Small Businesses
Runs a firm that places and supports fractional CFOs in small businesses. Stays involved even after placing a full-time CFO — views the relationship as ongoing partnership, not a transaction.

---

## The Origin Story

Dan came to fractional work through experience, not theory. He ran a previous business, sold it, and then got his CEPA (Certified Exit Planning) certification — in that order.

> "I got my CEPA after I sold the previous business, not before. Should have got it before."

He discovered EOS through the book Traction, which crystallized the mindset shift he now brings to every engagement.

> "You have a business to run and not a job to go to."

---

## What Makes Dan Remarkable

### 1. He Sees Human Transformation, Not Just Financial Metrics
Dan's most powerful story isn't about numbers. It's about a business owner who walked into their first meeting looking completely wrecked — hair everywhere, visibly overwhelmed, doing everything themselves. A few months later, that same person was calm, balanced, breathing.

> "I vividly remember a client, first meeting, came in and hair was all over the place and you looked like he'd been put through the wringer. And it was really only a few months later that it was in a much calmer place. And I could feel the difference. That to me, that was a successful engagement — when you can inject some balance in the owner's life."

### 2. He Stays Involved After Placing a CFO
Unlike many fractional firms that hand off and walk away, Dan's team stays engaged. When a client graduates to needing a full-time CFO, they help find one — and then keep supporting the business.

> "When we put a CFO in an organization, I get to stay involved. I'm fortunate that I don't put a CFO in and walk away. We stay involved as a team and help the business grow."

### 3. He's a Small Business Evangelist
Dan doesn't just work with small businesses — he believes they're the backbone of America. His conviction is genuine and deeply felt.

> "This country is run by small businesses and business owners. And to be able to go in and help and provide a resource that helps a business owner lead their team, lead their organization to higher levels — those are really rewarding experiences."

### 4. The Husband-and-Wife Transformation
A husband-and-wife team doing everything themselves — he was in the field, she was inside handling admin, working month to month (sometimes week to week) on financials. Dan's team brought CFO clarity, freed up cash flow, built banking relationships, shifted payroll, brought in HR services. The result: they went from having a job to owning a business.

> "There's balance in their life, as opposed to every minute of every day committed to going into the office and going to a job instead of owning a business."

---

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Balance over metrics:** The ultimate KPI is whether the business owner's life has gotten better, not just their P&L
- **Investment, not cost:** Aggressively reframes the fractional conversation — C-suite talent is accessible and ROI-positive for small businesses
- **Graduating clients:** When a client needs a CFO 3 days a week, Dan recommends they hire full-time. That's a win.
- **Start earlier:** His biggest regret is not starting fractional sooner. His advice to others: don't wait.
- **Referral culture:** If he's not the right fit, he'll connect you with whoever is. The community is the product.

> "Had I been aware of the fractional world and what it does, and had I had faith in myself that I could come out here and do this and help businesses in the way I have, I would have probably started earlier."
