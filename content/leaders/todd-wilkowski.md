# Todd Wilkowski — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional Trusted Advisor / Business Attorney** | Exit Planning, Roundtable Facilitation, Relationship Building
Works with middle market and small-to-medium sized businesses, equipping them to grow and ultimately exit. Runs roundtable groups. Connected to Vistage. Board member for the Conduit Apprenticeship Program (making trades accessible to graduating seniors).

---

## The Origin Story

Todd is a business guy who happened to go to law school — and that distinction matters to him. After 11 years in the fractional world, he has built a practice around trust, relationships, and helping business owners navigate growth and exit with their identity intact.

> "I'm not really a lawyer. I'm a business guy that happened to go to law school. Big difference, right?"

His path into fractional work was not a gradual transition — it was a full commitment. He learned early that half-measures do not work in this space.

> "You can't dip your toes in. You're either in or you're out."

---

## What Makes Todd Remarkable

### 1. Relationship-First in a Transactional World
In a profession built on billable hours and deal closings, Todd sees "transactional relationship" as an oxymoron. He insists on forming trust before doing business — and he has built his entire practice around that conviction.

> "Transactional relationship. Remember counter opposites? That's what that is. That's what we're trying to change."

### 2. The Connector Archetype — "The Consigliere"
Everyone defers to Todd as the person who brings people together. He is the one making introductions, seeing the connections others miss, and building the tissue between people and organizations. He has earned the title of consigliere — the trusted advisor behind the scenes.

> "I just see cross-pollination all over the place."

### 3. Deep Conviction About Identity Beyond Business
Todd pushes founders to confront the hardest question before they exit: who are you without your company? He has seen too many owners reach closing day with no answer.

> "The first thing I ask is, okay, you're thinking about exiting. What are you going to do day one after closing?"

> "Their identity is not that. And that's just it."

### 4. 11 Years of Hard-Won Fractional Wisdom
More than a decade in the fractional world has given Todd clarity that newer practitioners are still finding. He knows what works, what does not, and why commitment matters more than credentials.

> "We all have a passion for the middle market and equipping and empowering them to grow and ultimately to exit."

### 5. Sees the Whole Ecosystem
Todd operates at the intersection of exit planning, identity work, fractional placement, and relationship building. Where others see separate disciplines, he sees one connected system.

> "This makes my heart happy. Watching all this come together, it's pretty cool."

---

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Trust is the foundation:** Relationships must be formed first — before any business gets done
- **Identity work before exit:** Business owners need to do the personal work before closing, not after
- **Cross-pollination mindset:** Sees connections between people, disciplines, and organizations that others miss
- **Full commitment required:** 11 years have proven that fractional only works when you go all in
- **Ecosystem thinking:** Exit planning, identity, fractional placement, and relationship building are one system
