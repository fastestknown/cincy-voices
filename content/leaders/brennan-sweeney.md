# Brennan Sweeney — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional CFO** | Strategic Financial Advisory
Started his fractional journey after being inspired by his father's entrepreneurial leap from corporate to running a patio furniture business. Brennan himself started, grew, and exited a local HOA property management company before moving into fractional work.

---

## The Origin Story

Brennan watched his father step away from a large corporate role to start his own patio furniture business. That moment planted the seed.

> "Seeing my father step from a large corporate role was really inspiring. I kind of find myself following that same journey of entrepreneurship — where I was able to start, grow, and exit from a local HOA property management company."

---

## What Makes Brennan Remarkable

### 1. He Refers Away Work to Build Trust
Brennan's defining story is about a brewery client who came to him early on. He realized the brewery needed a deeper financial dive — closer to an audit — while his strength was strategic, forward-looking advisory. Instead of taking the work, he referred them to someone better suited.

> "I was able to say, I'm not the right person. I may be able to help you in the future, but let me introduce you to someone that can deliver what you need today."

The brewery came back later when they'd grown into the kind of work Brennan excels at. They remembered the trust.

> "It was kind of the passing on the initial work because I wasn't the right fit. But then that company kind of grew to where I was the right fit. And I think they remembered that trust."

### 2. Trust Is His Foundation
Everything Brennan does starts with trust. It's not a buzzword for him — it's a practiced discipline of integrity that compounds over time.

> "Trust is critical, and you get nowhere with a client or a business without kind of earning that trust."

---

## Philosophy & Approach

- **Integrity over revenue:** Willing to walk away from work that's not the right fit, knowing it builds something more valuable than a single engagement
- **Long game:** Trusts that doing the right thing will create the right outcomes
- **Partnership model:** Views the fractional relationship as a true partnership, not a vendor arrangement
