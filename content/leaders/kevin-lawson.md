# Kevin Lawson — Leader Profile Reference

## Role & Identity
**Fractional VP of Sales** | Sales Systemization, Owner-to-Salesperson Transition, Revenue Operations
Works with under-resourced, under-systemized businesses that need to professionalize their sales function without losing the soul of what made them successful.

---

## The Origin Story

Kevin's understanding of business started at home. His father was a small business owner — an importer who held an exclusive US distributorship. Watching that operation up close planted the seed.

From there, Kevin went into Fortune 500 construction wholesale distribution, then moved to a family-owned business navigating a Gen 1-to-Gen 2 ownership transition, where he helped consolidate 35 ERPs. Then came a software company selling ERP to SMBs.

The through-line across every stop was the same: under-resourced, under-systemized businesses trying to compete against bigger, better-funded competitors. That pattern became his calling.

> "I want to help small businesses punch above their weight."

---

## What Makes Kevin Remarkable

### 1. Systems-and-Empathy Approach
Kevin leads with empathy, not heavy-handed corporate tactics. He understands that imposing change on a team already operating in chaos only creates more chaos. He earns trust first, then introduces structure.

> "If someone is operating in chaos, it is an incorrect assumption or expectation that somebody's going to feel great about change."

> "I use empathy as kind of my superpower to get them onto the plan and process of adopting change."

### 2. The Chick-fil-A Analogy for B2B
Kevin's North Star for sales organizations is the Chick-fil-A model: a consistent brand experience regardless of which touchpoint a customer hits. He brings that consumer-grade consistency to B2B companies that have never thought about their sales process as a brand experience.

> "Any business can operate at a higher level if we will codify and universally adopt a system for selling and attracting talent."

### 3. Enterprise-Wide Thinker Wearing a Sales Hat
Most sales leaders think about pipeline and close rates. Kevin thinks about how sales levers impact marketing, delivery, and accounting. He sees the whole organism, not just his function.

> "The enterprise is not just a sales function. The enterprise is a full-service delivery organization — an organism."

### 4. Specialist in Owner-to-First-Salesperson Transition
One of the hardest inflection points for any growing business is when the founder can no longer be the sole salesperson. Kevin specializes in navigating that transition — building the system, the role, and the handoff so the owner can let go without losing control.

### 5. Turnaround DNA — Process, Not Cuts
Kevin is a process guy. When he walks into a struggling operation, he extracts cost and drives impact through systems and controls, not layoffs and slash-and-burn tactics.

> "I was not a hatchet man, I was a process guy."

---

## Philosophy & Approach

- **"Systems and controls":** Systems = what we do. Controls = how we measure. This framework governs everything Kevin builds.
- **Chess not checkers:** Think multiple moves ahead. Nest inside the customer's success model and work backwards.
- **Empathy as superpower:** Change management starts with understanding where people are, not where you want them to be.
- **Think downstream:** Don't just close the deal — understand how the customer succeeds after the sale, and build that understanding into the sales process.
- **Accessible but not interim:** Kevin embeds as a real leader, not a temp filling a seat.

> "I'm accessible but not interim."
