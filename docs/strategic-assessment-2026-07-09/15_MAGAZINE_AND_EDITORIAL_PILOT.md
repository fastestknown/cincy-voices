# Magazine and Editorial Pilot

## Decision: later, not now, not never

Cincy Voices footage should **not** feed the magazine right now. The magazine strategy report is explicit that the magazine is a credibility asset and relationship engine, not a profit center, with a recommended first-year budget of $55-65K and a 3-year horizon before it could be a standalone business. That means the magazine itself is still in early validation. Bolting a second unproven pipeline (footage-to-feature) onto a still-unproven publication is a compounding-risk move, not a leverage move.

## The evidence for a future connection is real, but it was never designed

Both magazine prototypes (issue-00 and issue-00-v2) feature six subjects who also have Cincy Voices footage: Meredith Arlinghaus, Will Phillips, Brennan Sweeney, Tobias Brauer, Carter Varn, Todd Wilkowski. That is not a coincidence, it reflects the same relationship network. But no document anywhere states that the magazine profiles were written from Cincy Voices transcripts, and a full-text search of the magazine strategy report for any Cincy Voices reference returned zero hits. These were built as two separate workstreams that happen to share subjects, not as one pipeline.

## Treat the magazine as a credibility asset and relationship engine, not a direct profit center

This framing (already established in the existing strategy report) should govern any future Cincy Voices connection too. If footage ever feeds a magazine feature, the goal is depth and trust with that one leader and their network, not volume of content produced. This argues for extreme selectivity if this path is ever pursued: one or two features sourced from the richest existing interviews (Will Phillips, Amy Sheehy, Christine Bell — the three furthest along in the Cincy Voices pipeline), not a blanket policy of turning every transcript into a feature.

## Minimum viable editorial artifact before committing to print

Before any print commitment: one digital-only feature, written from Cincy Voices transcript evidence, reviewed and approved by both Ford and the subject leader, published on the WWM site or as a standalone piece (not yet in a print magazine issue). If that single piece performs well (leader is proud to share it, generates a real conversation or lead, doesn't require unusual effort to produce), that's the signal to consider a print feature. If it's a struggle to produce or the leader is lukewarm, that's the signal to stop before investing further.

## Pilot issue or digital feature concept (only because there is evidence to support at least trying one)

**Concept:** a single digital "Conversation" piece (matching the existing issue-00 format) built from the Will Phillips Cincy Voices interview, since he has the most complete pipeline coverage (multiple staged clips, Gemini-QA'd, an existing rich markdown profile). Working title direction, in Ford's voice, not corporate: something built around the "outsider questions" theme already present in both his Cincy Voices clip set and his existing profile's "Power Quotes" section.

**Gate:** this is explicitly optional, not part of the 7-day or 30-day critical path. It should only proceed if the profile-enrichment work (higher priority, see `14_PROFILE_ENRICHMENT_MODEL.md`) is going well and there's real bandwidth, and if Will Phillips reviews and is genuinely enthusiastic about being featured, not just polite about it.

## What this decision protects against

The temptation, under the "more content is more leverage" instinct this assessment explicitly warns against, to treat 15 rich leader profiles and hours of footage as raw material for 15 magazine features. That would be a giant content factory, not a credibility asset. One real feature, done well, with a leader who's genuinely proud of it, is worth more than five rushed ones.
