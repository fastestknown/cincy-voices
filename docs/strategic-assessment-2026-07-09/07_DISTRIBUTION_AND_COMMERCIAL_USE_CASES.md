# Distribution and Commercial Use Cases

All external-facing items below are **draft only, not send-ready**, until Ford approves and, where a leader is featured, until the leader has reviewed it.

## 1. Leader vault delivery
- **Audience:** the 17 filmed leaders.
- **Inputs:** staged/approved clips for that leader, existing vault magic-link infrastructure.
- **Output example:** "Your Cincy Voices clips are ready" email with a magic link.
- **Review gate:** Ford. **Consent gate:** implicit from filming, but confirm before including any clip flagged sensitive.
- **Expected value:** near-zero cost, directly strengthens the relationship, closes an already-built feature loop.

## 2. Leader-owned LinkedIn kit
- **Audience:** the leader, for their own posting.
- **Inputs:** 1-2 approved clips + a draft caption per leader.
- **Output example:** downloadable clip + suggested caption text (leader edits as they wish).
- **Review gate:** Ford drafts, leader has final say on their own use.
- **Consent gate:** explicit, leader opts in to receiving/using the kit.
- **Expected value:** high goodwill, indirect WWM visibility via the leader's own network.

## 3. Buyer-education objection-handling reel
- **Audience:** SMB owners evaluating fractional leadership.
- **Inputs:** clips scoring ≥7 on buyer relevance, organized by common objection.
- **Output example:** a 3-4 clip sequence answering "how do I know this person will actually show up."
- **Review gate:** Ford + Content Strategist. **Consent gate:** explicit per leader for this specific use.
- **Expected value:** high — this is differentiated proof most competitors don't have.

## 4. Sales collateral one-pager
- **Audience:** prospects in active WWM conversations.
- **Inputs:** enriched leader profile + one power quote + one clip thumbnail.
- **Output example:** a leader proof-point sheet attached to a proposal.
- **Review gate:** Ford, per use (Rule 101, client-facing deliverable review).
- **Consent gate:** explicit, this is a commercial use of the leader's name and likeness.
- **Expected value:** high, directly supports deals in motion.

## 5. Roebling demo clip
- **Audience:** internal, or controlled prospect demos only.
- **Inputs:** one enriched profile showing video-derived fields flowing into a WWM Platform profile.
- **Output example:** a screen-recorded walkthrough.
- **Review gate:** Ford, explicit approval required before any external showing (per the master prompt's instruction to treat Roebling demos as internal/controlled unless explicitly approved for public use).
- **Consent gate:** internal use needs none; any prospect-facing use needs leader consent.
- **Expected value:** medium-high, good proof of the AI-enablement thesis, but narrow audience.

## 6. WWM Platform profile enrichment (internal)
- **Audience:** internal (Ford, future WWM Platform users).
- **Inputs:** Cincy Voices transcripts + leader-agent bridge.
- **Output example:** a richer `leaders` record than a text-intake-only profile would produce.
- **Review gate:** Ford approves each profile at `pending_review`.
- **Consent gate:** none for internal use; consent required before that profile data is shown to a client or prospect.
- **Expected value:** highest of all listed use cases — durable, reusable, compounding.

## 7. Referral nurture / event follow-up
- **Audience:** the leaders who attended the original event.
- **Inputs:** their finished clip + a short personal note.
- **Output example:** "here's your clip from Cincy Voices" follow-up email.
- **Review gate:** Ford (draft approval required per standing rule).
- **Consent gate:** none beyond original filming consent.
- **Expected value:** medium, relationship maintenance more than new pipeline.

## 8. Website editorial feature
- **Audience:** public, site visitors.
- **Inputs:** leader markdown profile + Content Strategist draft.
- **Output example:** a second editorial piece, matching the existing Kevin Lawson format.
- **Review gate:** Ford + leader (subject of the piece).
- **Consent gate:** explicit, public publication.
- **Expected value:** medium, content-marketing value, not differentiated versus generic thought leadership.

## 9. Internal relationship intelligence (Open Brain)
- **Audience:** Jun / Ford, internally.
- **Inputs:** approved segments/quotes already flowing via `embed-approved.sh`.
- **Output example:** Jun recalling a leader's stated positioning or a prior quote during future outreach planning.
- **Review gate:** none needed for internal recall use; flag before this surfaces in anything client-facing.
- **Consent gate:** none for internal use.
- **Expected value:** medium, quiet compounding value, hard to measure directly.

## 10. WWM magazine feature (future, not now)
- **Audience:** magazine readers (credibility/relationship audience, not a mass audience).
- **Inputs:** would require a new bridge from Cincy Voices transcripts to a magazine-feature draft.
- **Output example:** a "Conversation" or "Long Read" piece in the existing Practice format.
- **Review gate:** Ford + leader, higher bar than social given permanence and print.
- **Consent gate:** explicit, and should be the strongest consent bar of any use case here given the permanence of print.
- **Expected value:** speculative — real but not urgent; see `15_MAGAZINE_AND_EDITORIAL_PILOT.md` for the full reasoning on why this waits.

## 11. Cincinnati fractional-leadership community/event teaser (bonus, 11th case)
- **Audience:** prospective attendees of a future Cincy Voices event.
- **Inputs:** the existing supercut trailer and themed reels (already produced, April).
- **Output example:** reuse the existing trailer as an invite/teaser for a second event.
- **Review gate:** Ford. **Consent gate:** confirm original consent covers reuse as a future-event promo, don't assume it does.
- **Expected value:** medium, this is genuinely low-effort since the asset already exists.
